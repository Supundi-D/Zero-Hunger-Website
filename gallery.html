Eradicating Hunger

Ensuring no one goes to bed hungry by providing access to sufficient food for all, particularly for the most vulnerable populations.

Food Security

Guaranteeing consistent and reliable access to food by stabilizing food supplies and improving resilience against economic, social, and environmental disruptions.

Improving Nutrition

Enhancing the quality of diets to ensure adequate intake of essential nutrients, reducing malnutrition and related health issues.

Sustainable Agriculture

Promoting farming practices that maintain and restore ecosystems, strengthen the capacity to adapt to climate change, and improve the productivity and incomes of small-scale farmers.

Support Local Farmers

Investing in small-scale farmers and providing them with the tools and knowledge to improve productivity and sustainability.

Innovative Technologies

Implementing advanced agricultural techniques and technologies to boost food production and reduce waste.

Improved Food Distribution

Enhancing infrastructure and logistics to ensure food reaches those in need efficiently.

Education and Awareness

Promoting nutritional education to encourage healthy eating habits and reduce food waste.

Food Rescue Program

Food Recovery and Redistribution is a core aspect of the Zero Hunger.This effort aims to tackle food insecurity by redirecting surplus food from stores and other facilities to those in need, instead of allowing it to go to waste.
